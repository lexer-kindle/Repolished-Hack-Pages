What You Will Need

Setting up a DS for flashcard use takes very little. There is no exploit chain and no risk of bricking the console, so the "requirements" are really just a short shopping and preparation list. Gather these items before you start.

  • A slot-1 flashcard — a reputable model such as a genuine R4i-SDHC, an Ace3DS+, a DSTT, or a SuperCard DSTwo if you want extra emulation. Buy from a seller cross-checked against a current community compatibility list to avoid counterfeits.
  • A microSD card — most modern cards accept SDHC capacities. Cards of 4 GB to 32 GB are plenty for DS games and homebrew. Stick with a known brand; fake high-capacity cards are a common cause of corruption.
  • A microSD reader or adapter — a USB reader or a full-size SD adapter so your PC can write files to the card.
  • A computer — Windows, macOS, or Linux all work. You only need it to format the card and copy files.
  • The correct kernel files — the flashcard's operating software, downloaded from the maker's official site for your exact card and revision. Using the wrong kernel is the number-one reason a card refuses to boot.
  • Your DS or DS Lite — fully charged or plugged in.

For optional hardware mods you will also want a fine Phillips and tri-wing screwdriver set, a plastic spudger or pry tool, and, for screen swaps, a low-wattage soldering iron with flux and fine solder. Those tools are only needed if you plan to open the shell.

Format the microSD as FAT32 before copying anything. Most flashcards expect FAT32 and will not see an exFAT or NTFS card, so confirm the filesystem first.
