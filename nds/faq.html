Nintendo DS

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I brick my DS by using a flashcard?

No. Flashcards do not modify the console's firmware — they are just cartridges the DS reads like any game. If something goes wrong you simply remove the card. The only real brick risk on a DS comes from hardware mods that involve opening the shell, not from anything on the card.

Do I need a donor cartridge or an exploit?

Not with a modern card. Every "true" slot-1 flashcard sold today has NoPass built in, so you insert it, power on, and it boots straight to its own menu. The old PassMe-style donor tricks are history.

Which card should I buy?

For DS games and homebrew, a genuine R4i-SDHC, an Ace3DS+, or a budget DSTT all do the job. If you want SNES, Genesis, or PlayStation emulation on the DS itself, choose the SuperCard DSTwo, whose onboard CPU makes that possible.

Does it matter if I have an original DS or a DS Lite?

For flashcards, no — slot-1 support is identical. The Lite is brighter, slimmer, and the better target for screen swaps. The original DS is bulkier but keeps the same slot-2 GBA port the Lite has.

What microSD format do I need?

Format the card as FAT32. Most flashcards cannot read exFAT or NTFS. A reputable 4–32 GB card is plenty.

Why did my cheap card stop working?

It is probably a clone. Counterfeits often die after a date change or when their unofficial servers vanish. Buy genuine cards cross-checked against a community compatibility list.

Can the DS emulate the SNES on its own?

Not natively — the DS lacks the power. Only the DSTwo's dedicated hardware makes good SNES, Genesis, and PlayStation emulation practical.
