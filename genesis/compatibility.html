Compatibility

Console Models and Region Compatibility

Identifying your hardware is the first real step in any Genesis project. Sega produced three major revisions, and each one behaves differently when modified. Flip your console over and check the model number on the bottom label, then note whether it says Genesis (North America), Mega Drive (Europe, Japan, and most other regions), or something else.

The Model 1 is the large, original unit. North American Model 1 consoles came in "high definition graphics" and later variants; the very earliest "VA0" boards have the best audio and the most internal space. Model 1 outputs strong RGB, supports stereo audio at the headphone jack, and is the easiest target for region switches, 50/60Hz mods, S-Video, overclocking, and LED swaps. If you are buying a console specifically to mod, look for a Model 1.

The Model 2 is smaller and cheaper to find. It still outputs solid RGB and stereo audio through the multi-out, and it accepts the same region and refresh mods, but the tighter case leaves less room for extra boards. The Model 3, made later by third parties, is the most compact. It does output RGB, but its audio is mono only, which makes it a poor choice if sound quality matters to you.

Region matters for two reasons: physical cartridge shape and software lockout. Japanese Mega Drive carts are a slightly different shape and will not fit a North American slot without widening the plastic. Software region checks and the 50Hz/60Hz difference are handled electronically by the region mod.
