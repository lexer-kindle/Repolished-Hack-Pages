Sega Genesis

Installation

Disclaimer: Modifying your console carries real risk. Soldering can damage the board, lift traces, or destroy components, and any internal work voids whatever warranty might remain. You perform these steps entirely at your own risk. If you have never soldered before, practice on a scrap board first, and never work on a powered console. Always confirm your model and region before starting, since the wrong procedure on the wrong hardware can ruin it.

This walkthrough covers the switchless region and 50/60Hz mod, the most popular soldered upgrade. RGB cables and flash carts need no installation beyond plugging them in. Read every phase before touching the iron.

  1. Identify and plan. Confirm whether you have a Model 1, 2, or 3 and your region. Find the firmware build of MegaDrivePlusPlus or equivalent that matches your board revision, and note exactly which signal points it expects. Different board revisions place these pads in different spots, so matching documentation to your specific board is essential.
  2. Open the case. Power down, unplug everything, and remove the shell screws, using a gamebit if your unit has security screws. Lift the top half gently, watching for any ribbon cables or the RF shield. Set screws aside in a labeled container so reassembly is painless.
  3. Locate the signal points. Using your documentation and a multimeter, find the jp1/jp2 region pads, the relevant CPU clock or region-select lines, the Reset line, and a stable ground and power point. Confirm each with continuity testing before you commit any solder. Measuring twice here prevents the most common mistakes.
  4. Wire the chip. Mount the programmable chip, then run thin enameled wire from each of its pins to the points you verified. Keep wires short and tidy, and tack them down so they cannot snag. Solder the power and ground first, then the region and refresh lines, then the Reset line that enables In-Game-Reset and mode cycling.
  5. Connect the LED feedback. If your firmware drives the power LED to show the active region, wire it according to the build's instructions. This gives you a visible indicator of whether the console is in Japanese, North American, or European mode.
  6. Inspect before powering. Check every joint under good light for bridges or cold solder. Re-test continuity and confirm there are no shorts between power and ground. A careful inspection now beats chasing a fault later.
  7. Test outside the case. With the board still accessible, power on with a known game. Verify it boots, then hold Reset to cycle modes and confirm the LED and refresh rate change. Try a foreign game if you have one to confirm region switching.
  8. Reassemble. Once everything works, route wires clear of the cartridge slot and screw holes, then close the shell. Reinstall every screw and test one final time fully assembled.

If anything misbehaves, stop and revisit the troubleshooting page rather than forcing it. Patience at each phase is what separates a clean mod from a dead console.
