Sega Genesis

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Which model should I buy to mod?

The Model 1 is the clear winner. It outputs the best RGB, has stereo audio at the headphone jack, the most internal room, and the widest mod support including S-Video and overclocking. The Model 2 is a fine second choice. The Model 3 outputs RGB but only in mono audio, so it is the least desirable for a full build.

Do I need to solder anything to get RGB?

Usually not. The console already outputs raw RGB from its multi-out. You just need a proper Sega RGB SCART cable that includes the 75-ohm resistors and coupling capacitors on each color line. The components live in the cable, not the console.

What does the switchless region mod actually do?

It lets you cycle between Japanese, North American, and European modes, and between 50Hz and 60Hz, by holding the Reset button, with no toggle switches drilled into the shell. Firmware like MegaDrivePlusPlus also adds In-Game-Reset and often uses the power LED to show the active mode.

Can I play Sega CD games without the Sega CD?

Yes. Both the Mega EverDrive Pro and the Terraonion Mega SD emulate the Sega CD entirely within the cartridge, so you can run CD titles from disc images without the original add-on hardware.

Why won't my Japanese cartridge fit?

Japanese Mega Drive carts are shaped slightly differently. On a Model 1 you can trim two small plastic tabs inside the slot so they seat fully. This is a plastic-only change and does not touch the board.

Do flash carts get around region lockout?

Yes. Flash carts handle region differences in software, so foreign games typically just run. A switchless region mod is mainly useful for playing original cartridges from other regions.

Is overclocking worth it?

For most people, no. It is a niche tweak best done on a Model 1, offering a small speed bump that can introduce compatibility quirks. RGB and a flash cart deliver far more everyday value.
