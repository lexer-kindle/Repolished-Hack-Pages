Compatibility

Compatibility and Boot ROM Check

Before choosing a method, you need to know your console's internal boot ROM version. This single number decides whether the classic FreeMcBoot (FMCB) route is available to you or whether you must take the FreeDVDBoot path instead. The easiest way to read it is to first get any temporary homebrew booting and open uLaunchELF, which displays the ROM version on screen.

The key fact: FMCB does not work on boot ROM 2.30 or 2.50. These late-revision ROMs ship on most R-chassis SCPH-90000 slim consoles and on the PSX-style PS2 TV units. If your console reports one of those versions, skip FMCB entirely and use FreeDVDBoot, then install a persistent memory-card exploit afterward.

Model differences matter too. Fat (phat) consoles such as the SCPH-30000 through SCPH-50000 series support an expansion bay and internal IDE hard drives, making them ideal for HDD-based game loading. Slim models (SCPH-70000 onward) are smaller and lighter but have no internal HDD bay, so they rely on USB, MX4SIO SD adapters, or network loading. Almost every console can use a memory card and a DVD drive, so at least one boot method is available to nearly everyone.

If you are unsure of your model, the SCPH number is printed on a label on the underside of the console. Confirm the boot ROM in uLaunchELF before committing to a route, since guessing wrong wastes time and burned discs.
