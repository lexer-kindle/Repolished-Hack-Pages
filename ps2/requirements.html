What You'll Need

Gathering the right gear before you start saves a lot of frustration. The exact list depends on which boot method your console's ROM version allows and how you plan to load games, but the essentials are modest and inexpensive.

  • A genuine PS2 memory card (8MB). This is the heart of an FMCB, PS2BBL, or OpenTuna install, since the exploit lives in its save data. Authentic Sony cards are the most reliable; many clone cards behave inconsistently. Even on the FreeDVDBoot route you will want a memory card to hold a persistent exploit afterward.
  • A blank DVD-R, if you need FreeDVDBoot. Consoles on boot ROM 2.30 or 2.50 cannot use FMCB, so you will burn a FreeDVDBoot image to a quality DVD-R (not DVD+R) at a slow write speed for best compatibility.
  • A storage device for your games. Pick one that suits your console: a USB flash drive (FAT32, ideally USB 2.0-class), an SD card with an MX4SIO adapter that plugs into the memory-card slot, an internal IDE hard drive for fat models with an expansion bay, or an SMB network share for loading over a wired LAN.
  • A PC. You will need a computer to download the homebrew files, format and copy data to your USB drive or SD card, and burn a DVD-R if required. A built-in or USB card reader helps for memory-card prep tools.
  • A controller and the console itself, plus the standard AV and power cables. A network adapter (built into slims, an expansion-bay module on fats) is needed only for SMB or online HDD setups.

Optional but useful extras include a USB-to-memory-card adapter for backing up the card from your PC, and a second memory card so you can keep your exploit separate from your game saves. With these in hand you are ready to pick a method.
