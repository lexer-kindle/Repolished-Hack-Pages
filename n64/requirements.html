What you'll need

What You Will Need

Your shopping list depends on which of the three modding goals you are chasing. Gather only what applies to your plan; nothing here is mandatory for all three.

  • A flashcart if you want SD-based loading. Pick the EverDrive-64 X7 for save backup and USB, the EverDrive-64 X5 to save money, or the open-source SummerCart64 for 64DD emulation and automatic save backup.
  • A microSD or SD card sized for the flashcart you chose. A modest card holds the entire commercial library; format it FAT32 unless the cart documentation says otherwise.
  • A card reader and a computer to copy ROMs, homebrew, and menu firmware onto the card.
  • For imports between NTSC-U and NTSC-J: a pair of flush cutters or a small flat screwdriver to trim the plastic cartridge tabs, plus a gamebit-style security bit if you intend to open shells.
  • For PAL crossover: a passthrough adapter such as the N64 Passport, or a donor console PIF chip and soldering gear for a permanent fix.
  • For an HDMI mod: an UltraHDMI or PixelFX kit matched to your console, a fine-tip soldering iron, flux, and patience for the ribbon connections.
  • For native RGB: the appropriate amplifier kit for your board revision and basic soldering tools.
  • General tools: a Phillips and gamebit driver set, isopropyl alcohol, and good lighting.

If you are only running a flashcart, the first three items are all you need. Video and region work add tools but never remove the simplicity of the flashcart path.
