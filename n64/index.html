Nintendo 64 Hacks Guide

A cartridge console with no firmware, where modding means flashcarts, import play, and sharp RGB/HDMI video.

What this is for

The N64 has no updatable firmware, so modding covers flashcarts for homebrew and backups, region-free mods for imports, and video upgrades. Flashcarts like the EverDrive-64 and SummerCart64 load ROMs from SD and even emulate the 64DD. RGB and HDMI mods replace the soft composite output with crisp digital video.

What to check first: The N64 has no firmware to update. What matters is region (NTSC-U, NTSC-J, PAL) and motherboard revision: NTSC-U/J share the PIF chip so imports are largely tab-removal only, while PAL differs at the PIF and TV standard. RGB-mod difficulty depends on the internal board revision; HDMI mods work across all models.

⚠️ Before you start: Console modding carries real risk — an interrupted install or the wrong method/file for your exact model can leave hardware unbootable. Identify your model first, back up anything important, and proceed at your own risk.

What you'll need

  • An N64 console (note its region and, for video mods, its board revision)
  • A flashcart (EverDrive-64 X7/X5 or SummerCart64) plus a microSD/SD card
  • ROM files you are legally entitled to (your own backups)
  • Soldering iron and basic tools (only for region or RGB/HDMI mods)
  • An HDMI or RGB mod kit if upgrading video

How it works — the process

This walkthrough names the tools at each stage so you know what each one is and what it's for. Always use the latest release of each named tool.

  1. Choose your goal
    Decide between plug-and-play software (flashcart), import play (region mod), and better video (RGB/HDMI) - these combine.
  2. Flashcart setup
    Format an SD card, copy ROMs and the menu, and insert an EverDrive-64 or SummerCart64 - no soldering.
  3. Region-free (optional)
    For NTSC-U/J imports, trim the cart tabs. For PAL cross-play, add a passthrough or do a donor-PIF mod.
  4. Video upgrade (optional)
    Install an HDMI board (UltraHDMI/PixelFX) or an RGB amp mod; requires opening the console and soldering.
  5. Test and verify
    Boot the flashcart menu, confirm games load and saves persist, and verify clean video before reassembling.

Which option should I pick?

For most people the EverDrive-64 X7 (or X5 to save money) is the easiest all-in-one; pick SummerCart64 if you want open-source hardware, 64DD emulation, and auto save backup.
