Compatibility

Console Compatibility

Every GameCube can run homebrew, but the model and revision of your console decide how you get there. The two main retail models are the DOL-001 and the DOL-101, and the difference matters when picking an entry point.

The DOL-001 is the original launch model. It includes the high-speed Serial Port 2 on the underside and a digital AV output port for component/digital video. The presence of Serial Port 2 is what enables the popular SD2SP2 adapter. Note one wrinkle: a small number of late DOL-001 boards (sometimes called rev 1.1) had the Serial Port 2 connector omitted from the board, so SD2SP2 will not work on those units even though they are labeled DOL-001.

The DOL-101 is the cost-reduced later model. It removed the digital AV output, so analog-only. Crucially for homebrew, it also lacks usable Serial Port 2 access, meaning SD2SP2 is not an option. DOL-101 owners typically rely on a memory-card-slot adapter, a save exploit, PicoBoot, or an ODE instead.

If you are unsure which model you have, check the bottom of the console for the DOL-001 or DOL-101 label, and look for the rectangular digital AV port beside the analog port — its presence confirms a DOL-001. To confirm Serial Port 2, look for the removable cover door on the underside near the memory card slots.
