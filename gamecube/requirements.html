What you'll need

What You Will Need

Before starting, gather the hardware and software below. The exact list depends on which entry point you choose, but a few items are common to almost every setup.

  • A GameCube console — DOL-001 or DOL-101. Confirm your model first, since it limits which methods are available (see the Compatibility page).
  • Swiss — the all-in-one open-source loader. Download the latest .dol build. This is the program everything else launches.
  • An SD card formatted to FAT32 — Swiss reads files from FAT32. Most cards up to 32 GB work cleanly; larger cards should be reformatted to FAT32 rather than exFAT. A name-brand card is far more reliable than a cheap no-name one.
  • An entry point device — depending on your chosen method, this could be an SD2SP2 adapter (DOL-001 only), an SD Media Launcher or SD Gecko for the memory card slot, a PicoBoot (Raspberry Pi Pico) modchip, or an ODE such as GCLoader.
  • A GameCube memory card — required for save-exploit entry points (the hacked save lives here) and useful generally for managing saves with GCMM.
  • A computer with an SD card reader — to format the card and copy Swiss and your homebrew onto it.
  • A controller — needed to navigate Swiss and many homebrew apps.
  • For PicoBoot: a Raspberry Pi Pico (around $4), a soldering iron, thin wire, and a steady hand for roughly five connections.
  • Optional tools: GCMM for save/memory-card management, and a USB drive or SD-to-IDE setup if you plan to store larger game backups.

Have everything ready before you begin so you are not stuck mid-install. If you plan to solder PicoBoot, practice on scrap first.
