GameCube

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I brick my GameCube with homebrew?

Software-wise, the GameCube is very forgiving — it has no system firmware to corrupt, so a bad SD card or save simply fails to boot rather than bricking the console. The real risk is physical: soldering PicoBoot or installing an ODE incorrectly can damage hardware. Solder-free methods carry almost no risk.

Do I need to update any system software first?

No. The GameCube has no updatable firmware and no online accounts, so there is nothing to update and nothing to patch before you begin.

Which model is better for homebrew, DOL-001 or DOL-101?

The DOL-001 is more flexible because it has Serial Port 2 (for SD2SP2) and a digital AV port. The DOL-101 still works fine with save exploits, memory-slot SD adapters, PicoBoot, or an ODE, but it cannot use SD2SP2.

What is Swiss, exactly?

Swiss is the free, open-source, all-in-one homebrew loader for the GameCube. It launches .dol and .elf programs, browses your SD card, and boots game backups. Almost everything in the scene runs through it.

Do I need to solder anything?

Only for PicoBoot (about five wires) and for installing an ODE. Save exploits, SD2SP2, and SD Media Launcher / SD Gecko require no soldering at all.

Can I play my game backups from an SD card?

Yes. Swiss can boot ISO backups from FAT32 SD, USB, or an ODE like GCLoader. Only back up discs you legally own.

What format does my SD card need to be?

FAT32. Cards larger than 32 GB often ship as exFAT and must be reformatted to FAT32 to work with Swiss.
