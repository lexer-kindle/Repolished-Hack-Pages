Troubleshooting

Most GameCube homebrew problems come down to the SD card, a loose connection, or a small mistake in file placement. Work through the common issues below before assuming anything is broken.

Swiss Won't Load

If Swiss fails to start, first confirm the .dol file is the correct, current build and is named and placed where your entry point expects it. For a save exploit, make sure you selected the right hacked save and are using a supported game. For PicoBoot, a Swiss that never appears usually points to a soldering issue — recheck every wire and that the Pico is flashed correctly. For an SD adapter, reseat the adapter fully in its slot.

SD Card Not Detected

This is the most common complaint. Reformat the card to FAT32 (not exFAT or NTFS); cards over 32 GB often default to exFAT and must be reformatted manually. Try a smaller, name-brand card, since cheap or counterfeit cards frequently fail. Make sure the card is seated firmly. With SD2SP2, confirm your console is a DOL-001 and actually has the Serial Port 2 connector — some rev 1.1 boards omit it entirely.

Boot Fails or Console Hangs

A black screen or freeze during boot can indicate a bad hardware install. For PicoBoot, power down and inspect for cold solder joints, bridged pads, or a wire on the wrong point. For an ODE, confirm the drive was removed cleanly and the replacement board is seated and powered. If you recently changed video output, remember the DOL-101 has no digital AV port, so a digital cable will produce no picture on that model.

Homebrew Crashes or Won't Run

Verify the app is a GameCube build and not from another platform. Re-copy the file in case it was corrupted during transfer, and confirm any required ROMs or data files are in the folders the app expects. When in doubt, test with a known-good app like GCMM to isolate whether the problem is your setup or that specific program.
