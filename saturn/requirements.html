What you'll need

What You Will Need

The exact shopping list depends on which mod you choose, but the items below cover the common ground. Gather everything before you start so you are not stuck mid-install.

  • The mod itself — a Fenrir, Satiator, or Terraonion MODE ODE, or a Gamer's Cartridge for flashing Pseudo Saturn Kai. Match the Fenrir kit to your confirmed 20-pin or 21-pin ribbon.
  • A quality SD card — most ODEs read standard SD or microSD. Buy from a reputable brand and a known retailer; counterfeit cards are a leading cause of loading failures. A modest fast card outperforms a huge slow or fake one.
  • A small Phillips screwdriver — enough to remove the Saturn's shell and drive screws. A magnetized tip helps recover dropped fasteners.
  • A plastic spudger or guitar pick — for separating shell halves and lifting ribbon connectors without scratching plastic or cracking solder joints.
  • A clean, static-safe workspace — a well-lit table, a cup or tray for screws, and ideally an anti-static mat or wrist strap.
  • A way to manage game files — a computer to copy your legally owned backups onto the SD card in the format your ODE expects, such as BIN/CUE images for the Satiator.
  • A good RGB cable — the Saturn outputs strong native RGB, so a SCART or component-style cable plus a compatible display or upscaler transforms image quality. This is optional but highly recommended.
  • Patience and documentation — keep your vendor's wiring diagram or quick-start sheet open. Pin counts, headers, and mounting differ between kits.

Only the soldered ODE installs require any tools beyond a screwdriver. The Pseudo Saturn Kai cartridge route needs no opening of the console and no soldering at all — just the cartridge, a writer or supported method, and an SD card or CD-R for content.
