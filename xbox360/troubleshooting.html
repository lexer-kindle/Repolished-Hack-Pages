Troubleshooting

Most Xbox 360 modding problems trace back to a small number of causes. Work through them calmly and methodically rather than redoing the whole install.

The console will not boot into XeLL

On glitch methods, a few failed attempts before XeLL appears is normal, but if it never lands, suspect your wiring and timing setup first. Recheck every soldered point with a multimeter for continuity and shorts, confirm the glitch chip is configured for your exact board, and verify the Post Fix Adapter is present and seated on a Corona V3 or newer. Cold or bridged solder joints are the single most common culprit.

NAND dump errors or mismatches

If J-Runner reports a bad dump or your two reads do not match, do not flash anything. Re-seat the programmer connections, slow the read down if your tool allows it, and dump again until you get two identical, clean reads. A corrupt dump leads to a wrong CPU key and a non-working image.

Boot loops or freezing after flashing

If the console powers but hangs, the built image may not match the method or board, or the write may have been incomplete. Confirm you selected the correct method in J-Runner, that the CPU key is right, and re-flash from a verified image. Keep your original NAND backup so you can always return to a known state.

Console got patched or updated

If JTAG no longer applies because the dashboard was updated past the vulnerable point, switch to R-JTAG (for phats) or an RGH variant. Identify the current dashboard version before choosing.

Above all, keep backups of your original NAND dump and CPU key. With those, almost any mistake is recoverable. When you are stuck, slow down, verify one thing at a time, and consult a current board-specific reference for your motherboard.
