FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is installing custom firmware on my PSP safe?

CFW is widely regarded as one of the safer console mods. The standard install runs entirely from the Memory Stick with no soldering or case opening. As long as you stay on a charger, use files that match firmware 6.61, and follow the steps in order, the risk of a permanent brick is very low.

Which CFW should I install in 2026?

Use ARK-4. It is the actively maintained project, runs on every model on 6.61 including the Go and the Street (E1000), and is lightweight. Pair it with 6.61 Infinity to make it permanent. The older 6.61 PRO still works but is no longer the primary recommendation.

Do I have to be on firmware 6.61?

Yes. 6.61 is the final official firmware and the target for modern CFW. If your PSP is on anything older, update it to 6.61 first using Sony's official update before installing CFW.

What is the difference between ARK-4 and 6.61 Infinity?

ARK-4 is the custom firmware itself. 6.61 Infinity is not a separate CFW; it hooks the boot process so ARK-4 loads automatically on every startup instead of only when you launch it manually.

Will CFW remove my official games and save data?

No. CFW sits alongside the normal system. Your existing games, saves, and menus keep working exactly as before, and you gain homebrew and backup loading on top.

Do I need Pandora's Battery?

Only for recovery. It is a hardware method for unbricking a PSP-1000 (and some early 2000) or modding units stuck on very old firmware. A healthy PSP that can reach 6.61 does not need it.

Can the PSP Go and Street use CFW?

Yes. ARK-4 supports both. The Go uses internal storage, and the Street (E1000) works fully despite lacking Wi-Fi.
