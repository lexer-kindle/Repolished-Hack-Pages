Methods

Methods: CFW vs PS3HEN

The PS3 offers two distinct routes to homebrew. They reach a similar destination but differ in permanence, capability, and which consoles they support. Choose based on your model first, then on your preferences.

Custom Firmware (CFW)

CFW replaces the console's system software with a modified version that permanently unlocks homebrew. It is the most capable option and, once installed, it simply stays active: there is nothing to re-enable after a reboot. Well-maintained builds such as Evilnat keep up with the latest official firmware, so you are not stuck on old software.

Pros: permanent and always on; the broadest feature set; the best fit for power users who want everything available at every boot; mature, actively maintained builds.

Cons: only possible on hardware that is not factory-locked, meaning all Fat consoles and Slim models up to CECH-25xx. Later Slim and all Super Slim units cannot run CFW at all. Because it permanently alters system software, it deserves extra care and a calm, unhurried approach.

PS3HEN (Homebrew Enabler)

PS3HEN takes a lighter touch. Instead of replacing the firmware, it runs on top of HFW (Hybrid Firmware) and enables a homebrew-friendly state on demand. Crucially, it works on every model, which makes it the required path for later Slim (CECH-30xx) and all Super Slim (CECH-40xx) consoles that can never accept CFW.

Pros: universal model support; non-permanent and lower-commitment; runs on current firmware via HFW; a great choice for newer hardware or anyone who wants a more reversible setup.

Cons: it is temporary and must be re-enabled after each reboot, which is a minor but real inconvenience. A small number of advanced features available under full CFW are not present, though for the vast majority of homebrew the experience is very close.

How to Choose

If your console is CFW-capable (Fat, or Slim up to CECH-25xx), decide between maximum capability and permanence (CFW) or a lighter, reversible setup (PS3HEN). If your console is a later Slim or any Super Slim, the decision is made for you: PS3HEN is the path. Either way, both routes let you run the same popular homebrew managers and emulators, so you are not giving up the core experience by picking one over the other.
