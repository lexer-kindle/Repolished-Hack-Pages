Installation Overview

Disclaimer: This is a high-level educational overview, not a step-by-step exploit walkthrough. Modifying your console carries risk, including the possibility of an unusable system if steps are skipped or power is lost mid-process. Proceed only on hardware you own, for legal homebrew and personal backups. You accept full responsibility for the outcome. Read every stage completely before acting, and never interrupt a firmware operation.

The journey looks the same in spirit for both routes, with the firmware preparation differing depending on whether you are installing CFW or enabling PS3HEN. Work through the stages below in order.

  1. Identify your model and firmware. Read the CECH- code on the underside sticker and note your firmware under Settings > System > System Information. This confirms whether you are on the CFW path or the PS3HEN path before you change anything.
  2. Back everything up. Copy your save data, trophies, and account details to external storage. A good backup means that even in a worst case you have not lost what matters. Take this stage seriously.
  3. Choose your path. If your console is a Fat or a Slim up to CECH-25xx and you want maximum, permanent capability, choose CFW. If it is a later Slim (CECH-30xx) or any Super Slim (CECH-40xx), or you prefer a reversible setup, choose PS3HEN.
  4. Prepare the firmware. For the CFW path, you will prepare a maintained custom firmware build such as Evilnat that matches the current firmware series. For the PS3HEN path, you will prepare HFW (Hybrid Firmware), which keeps you on official-equivalent firmware while allowing the enabler to work. Place the correct package on your FAT32 USB drive.
  5. Apply the firmware. Using the console's standard system update mechanism, install the prepared firmware for your chosen path. This is the most sensitive stage: ensure stable power, do not touch the console while it works, and let it finish completely. CFW users are now permanently set up; PS3HEN users have laid the groundwork via HFW.
  6. Enable homebrew. CFW is active automatically once installed. For PS3HEN, run the enabler to switch the console into its homebrew state. Remember that PS3HEN is temporary and must be re-enabled after each reboot.
  7. Set up your apps. Install a homebrew manager such as webMAN MOD, multiMAN, or IRISMAN, then add the emulators and utilities you want. Verify everything launches before relying on it.

If any stage does not behave as expected, stop and consult the Troubleshooting page rather than improvising. A careful, methodical pace is what keeps this process safe.
