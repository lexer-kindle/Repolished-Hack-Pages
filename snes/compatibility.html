Compatibility

Compatibility: Board Type and Region

Two traits decide what your SNES needs: the motherboard revision and the region. Sort these out before ordering any parts, because the right RGB approach and the right region mod both depend on them.

1CHIP vs. older multi-chip boards

Early SNES and Super Famicom consoles use a multi-chip design, where the CPU and the two PPU video chips are separate packages. These boards produce a softer, slightly blurry RGB image. Later revisions consolidated everything into a single package. Boards marked SNS-CPU-1CHIP-01 or SNS-CPU-1CHIP-02 are the prized "1CHIP" units and output noticeably cleaner native RGB. The redesigned SNES Jr (board SNS-101) is technically 1CHIP internally but ships without working RGB on the multi-out, so it needs an amp to expose it.

To identify yours, open the shell and read the board silkscreen. A "1CHIP" string confirms the good board; anything else (SHVC- or SNS-CPU-RGB style markings without "1CHIP") is an older multi-chip unit.

Region

Region affects the lockout chip (CIC) and the video standard. NTSC consoles run at 60Hz, PAL at 50Hz. Imports may fail to boot due to lockout, or run at the wrong speed. A switchless region mod resolves both.
