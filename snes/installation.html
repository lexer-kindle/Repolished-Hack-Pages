Super Nintendo

Installation

Disclaimer: Opening your console and soldering to its board voids any warranty and carries real risk. A slipped iron, a bridged joint, or a reversed wire can permanently damage the SNES. The region and RGB steps below require steady soldering of fine wires. If you are not confident, fit only the FXPak Pro, which needs no soldering, and leave the internal work to an experienced installer. Work on a static-safe surface and double-check every connection before powering on.

Installation Walkthrough

Tackle the upgrades in order. The first phase needs no tools; the later phases involve opening the shell. Do not rush the identification step, since it determines what you actually fit.

  1. Identify the console. Open the shell with a 4.5mm gamebit driver and read the motherboard silkscreen. Confirm whether it says SNS-CPU-1CHIP-01/02 (clean native RGB), is an older multi-chip board (benefits from an amp), or is an SNS-101 / SNES Jr (requires an amp). Note the region too. Record these before ordering parts.
  2. Set up the flashcart. Format your microSD as FAT32, copy the current FXPak Pro firmware to it, then add your ROM backups in folders. Insert the card into the FXPak Pro and boot the console. Because the cart auto-patches region, this should work immediately on either an NTSC or PAL machine.
  3. Fit the region mod (optional). If you play original imports, install SuperCIC or Super CIC++. Solder the lockout, clock, reset, and LED lines per the board's wiring guide. After install, change region by holding Reset or entering the controller combo, and confirm the LED colour matches the selected mode.
  4. Perform the RGB upgrade. Skip this on a 1CHIP unit unless you want the marginal gain. On an older multi-chip board or an SNES Jr, mount a THS7374 bypass amp, tapping the raw RGB lines and routing the buffered output to the multi-out. Keep wire runs short and tidy to avoid noise.
  5. Cable and test. Reassemble, connect a quality RGB cable matched to your display or upscaler, and power on. Verify a stable, sharp picture with correct colour, test an import for region behaviour, and run several games from the flashcart, including a special-chip title and an MSU-1 hack, to confirm everything is solid.

If any step misbehaves, power down immediately and recheck your joints before continuing. The troubleshooting page covers the most common symptoms.
