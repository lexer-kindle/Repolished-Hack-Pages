Troubleshooting

Troubleshooting

Most problems trace back to one of three areas: import compatibility, video quality, or region switching. Work through the symptom that matches yours.

Imported cartridges will not boot

If an original import shows a black screen or refuses to start, the console's lockout is rejecting it, or the game expects a different video standard. On a machine with SuperCIC or Super CIC++, make sure you have actually switched to the correct region: hold Reset or enter the controller combo and watch the LED colour change. If you have no region mod, this is expected, and the FXPak Pro is the easier route for foreign software. Note that the FXPak Pro auto-patches region itself, so flashcart games should boot regardless.

Picture looks blurry or soft

A soft image is usually a board-and-cable issue, not a fault. Older multi-chip consoles are inherently softer over RGB; fitting a THS7374 bypass amp sharpens them considerably. If you own an SNES Jr (SNS-101) and get no RGB or a poor image, remember that this board needs an amp to output proper RGB at all. Also confirm your cable is a genuine RGB cable wired for your display, and that any sync option matches what your upscaler expects. A 1CHIP console with a good cable should already look crisp.

Region or 50/60Hz switching does nothing

If holding Reset or the controller combo has no effect, recheck the region mod's wiring, especially the lockout and reset lines and the LED connection used to indicate the mode. A combo that is entered too quickly or on the wrong controller port can also be ignored. Verify continuity with a multimeter and consult the mod's wiring guide.

FXPak Pro will not load games

Reformat the microSD as FAT32, reinstall the current firmware, and reseat the card. A corrupt firmware or an exotic card is the usual culprit.
