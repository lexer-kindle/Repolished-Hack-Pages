Original Xbox

Methods

Choosing a Modding Method

There are several ways to open up the original Xbox for homebrew. Each has different trade-offs in safety, permanence, and hardware requirements. Read all four below, then pick the one that matches your motherboard revision and comfort level.

ENDGAME Softmod (Recommended)

The ENDGAME softmod, delivered through Rocky5's Xbox Softmodding Tool, is the safest and most popular method. It is a save-based approach: you load a specially crafted game save that gains access to the system, then install a custom dashboard alongside the stock files. It requires no soldering, works on every motherboard revision from 1.0 to 1.6b, and is fully reversible. You can uninstall it cleanly and return the console to a stock state at any time. For the vast majority of users, this is the right choice and the one this guide focuses on.

TSOP Flashing

TSOP flashing writes a custom BIOS directly to the Xbox's onboard flash memory chip. This makes the modification persist independently of the hard drive and removes the need for a save file on every boot. However, it is only possible on revisions 1.0 through 1.5. The 1.6 and 1.6b boards use a flash chip that cannot be reprogrammed, so TSOP is off the table for them. TSOP also carries more risk than a softmod: a bad flash can leave the board needing recovery, and the method typically requires bridging points on the motherboard. It is best left to experienced users.

Modchips

A modchip is a small circuit board added to the Xbox that supplies its own BIOS at boot. Modern options like the OpenXenium and the Aladdin XT and XT Plus2 are popular because they are recovery-proof: even if the hard drive contents are wiped, the console still boots into a working modded environment. Modchips work on all revisions, including 1.6 and 1.6b, which makes them the go-to permanent solution for those later boards. The downside is that installation usually involves soldering or careful seating onto header points, so it demands more skill and the right tools.

HDD Upgrade

An HDD upgrade is not a modding method on its own but a common follow-up. Once the console is modded, you can replace the small original drive with a larger one to store more homebrew, emulators, and media. Because the drive is locked to the EEPROM key, you must have your EEPROM backed up and use the softmod tool's drive-preparation features to lock and format the new disk correctly. Do this only after a successful mod and a verified EEPROM backup.

Summary: choose the ENDGAME softmod if you want the safest reversible route on any board. Choose a modchip for a recovery-proof permanent setup, especially on 1.6 boards. Reserve TSOP for experienced users on 1.0 to 1.5 hardware.
