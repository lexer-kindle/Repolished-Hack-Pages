Homebrew & apps

Homebrew, Dashboards, and Media

Once your Xbox is modded, a whole world of community software opens up. The most visible change is the custom dashboard, the menu you see when the console boots. Two popular choices are XBMC4Gamers and EvoX. XBMC4Gamers is a polished, modern front end built on the legendary XBMC project, offering attractive game and app browsing along with media features. EvoX is a lightweight, classic dashboard prized for its simplicity and fast navigation.

From the dashboard you can launch emulators, which let you play games from older systems on hardware you own. The Xbox has a healthy library of community emulators covering many classic platforms. Always supply your own legally obtained content; this guide does not endorse downloading games you do not own.

The original Xbox also makes an excellent media player. With XBMC and its successors, you can stream or play video and music from a USB drive, a network share, or the console's own hard drive. Its standard-definition output suits a retro living-room setup, and it handles a wide range of file formats with ease.

Beyond dashboards and emulators, the homebrew scene offers original games, utilities, and tools built specifically for the platform by hobbyists. A larger hard drive, installed after modding, gives you room to store a full collection of applications and your personal media library in one place.

Everything described here is for legal, personal use: running open-source software, playing content you own, and enjoying media you have the rights to. The community thrives precisely because it keeps the hardware useful long after retail support ended.
