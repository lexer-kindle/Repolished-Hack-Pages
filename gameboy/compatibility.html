Compatibility

Console Compatibility

Before buying a single part, identify exactly which Game Boy you own. The three classic models share a family resemblance but differ in size, board layout, and screen dimensions, and almost every hardware mod is cut to fit one specific shell. Buying a kit for the wrong model is the most common and most frustrating mistake a first-time modder makes.

The DMG-01 is the original 1989 brick: large, grey, with a green-tinted reflective LCD and four AA batteries. The Pocket (MGB) is the slimmer 1996 redesign with a true black-and-white screen and a much smaller board. The Color (CGB) arrived in 1998 with a color reflective screen and a faster processor. Their displays are physically different sizes, so an IPS panel, a backlight film, and even a bivert polarizer must all be ordered for the correct console.

The good news for game loading: both the EverDrive-GB X7 and the EZ-Flash Junior work across all three consoles, since the cartridge slot is electrically the same. Color-only titles, however, will only run on a CGB. The screen and audio mods are where model matching is non-negotiable.

One DMG-specific caveat: fitting a modern IPS panel into the original shell usually requires shaving a small amount of plastic from the internal screen frame so the larger glass sits flush. Pocket and Color shells are generally drop-in once you have the right kit. Always confirm the kit's listed model designation, DMG, MGB, or CGB, matches your console before ordering.
