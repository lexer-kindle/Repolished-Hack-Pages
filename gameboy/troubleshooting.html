Troubleshooting

Troubleshooting

Most post-mod problems trace back to a loose connection, a mismatched part, or a solder slip. Work through these before assuming anything is permanently broken, and always reopen with the battery removed.

Blank or garbled screen after an IPS install

A black or scrambled display usually means the new panel's flex cable is not fully seated or is inserted backwards. Reopen, reseat the ribbon squarely in its connector, and confirm the kit is the correct model, a CGB panel forced into a DMG will never work. Check that no flex cable was creased during reassembly.

Lines, flicker, or screen tearing

Faint horizontal lines or tearing often come from a pinched ribbon or a panel sitting under slight pressure when the shell closes. Loosen the case screws and see if it clears; if so, reroute the wiring so nothing is compressed. With bivert installs, tearing or inverted-looking video can mean the hex inverter is wired to the wrong signal line, recheck that connection.

No sound or distorted audio

If the speaker is silent, inspect the new speaker's two solder joints and the amp wiring for a cold joint or a bridge. A buzzing or crackling output after a prosound mod usually points to a loose tap wire or a grounding issue, reflow the joint and confirm the ground is solid. Test headphone output separately to isolate whether the speaker or the signal is at fault.

Flashcart will not boot

A frozen logo or no menu is most often the SD card: reformat it, recopy the firmware and ROMs, and make sure the card is fully inserted. Clean the cart's contacts with isopropyl alcohol and reseat it firmly. Confirm the cart booted on the console before any hardware mod, so you know whether the cart or a mod is the cause.

When in doubt, undo your most recent step and retest. Isolating one change at a time is the fastest route to the fault.
