Methods: The Recap and Your Upgrade Paths

There is one mandatory job and several optional ones. We will cover the mandatory job first, because it genuinely is mandatory, then lay out the upgrades you can choose from once the console is healthy.

The Full Recap (Do This First)

Game Gear capacitors are surface-mount electrolytics, and over thirty-plus years they leak corrosive electrolyte that eats traces and kills audio and power circuits. A full recap means desoldering every electrolytic cap on the mainboard, power board, and audio board, cleaning the residue, and soldering in fresh replacements. Symptoms it cures: distorted, muffled, or absent sound; volume that is stuck low; a console that won't power on or dies after a moment; and dim or unstable picture. Kits from Console5, ZedLabz, and Retrosix include the correct values and polarities. This is the single highest-value thing you can do, and skipping it undermines every upgrade below.

McWill LCD Kit vs. IPS LCD Kit

The original Game Gear screen is dim, blurry, and prone to vertical line failures. Two replacement families dominate.

The McWill LCD kit has been the gold standard for years. It is a sharp digital panel that renders the picture crisply and adds genuinely useful features: multiple scaling modes and selectable scanlines that recall the original CRT-era look. It is the more expensive and more involved install, but the image quality and configurability are unmatched.

The IPS LCD kit is the cheaper, brighter alternative that has grown popular more recently. It delivers a vivid, high-contrast picture and is generally a simpler install. It does not always match the McWill's scaling and scanline flexibility, but for many people the lower price and excellent brightness make it the better value. Choose McWill for maximum tweakability, IPS for bright-and-affordable.

EverDrive-GG Flashcart

The EverDrive-GG turns a single cartridge into your whole collection. It plays Game Gear titles natively and, thanks to the hardware lineage, also runs Master System and SG-1000 software. Note that it typically ships as a bare board — you supply a donor cartridge shell or a 3D-printed case to house it. Load your ROMs onto an SD card, drop it in, and browse a menu instead of swapping carts.

Controller and AV Mods

Beyond the screen and storage, you can add an AV-out mod to send composite video to a TV or capture card, and controller mods that route an external gamepad to the Game Gear's inputs for comfortable tabletop play. These are small quality-of-life additions best done on the same bench session as your other upgrades — after the recap.
