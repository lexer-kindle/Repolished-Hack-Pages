What You Need Before Starting

Gather everything below before you begin. None of it is exotic, and most people already own the bulk of it. Taking a few minutes to line these up will save you from stopping halfway through activation.

  • An Xbox One, One S, One X, or Series console. Any model from the One family forward supports Developer Mode out of the box.
  • A Microsoft account. The same one you already use to sign in to your console and the Store is perfectly fine.
  • A free Partner Center developer registration. You attach this to your Microsoft account through a web browser. As of June 2025 individual onboarding costs nothing.
  • A computer or phone with a web browser. You will register the developer account and later operate the Device Portal from here, so it must be on the same network as the console.
  • A stable internet connection. Both the console and your computer should be online and, ideally, on the same local network for Device Portal access.
  • The Dev Mode Activation app. This is a free download from the Microsoft Store on the console itself. You install it after registering your account.
  • Storage space. Sideloaded apps and your emulator content need room. An external USB drive is strongly recommended, since emulator cores and game files add up quickly and individual apps are capped at 2 GB.
  • Your own legally obtained content. For emulators, that means homebrew, files you have personally dumped, or software you are licensed to use. This guide does not cover piracy and the platform cannot run pirated retail games.
  • A little patience. Account verification and the first activation can take a short while. Nothing here is instant, but nothing is difficult either.

With these in hand, you are ready to move on to the methods page, which explains exactly how Developer Mode fits together.
