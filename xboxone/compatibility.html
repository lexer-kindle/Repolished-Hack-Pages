Xbox One

Compatibility

Compatibility and Developer Mode Requirements

Developer Mode is supported across the entire Xbox One line, and the same approach carries forward to the newer Series consoles. The feature is baked into the system software, so you do not need a special hardware revision or a particular firmware version. What you do need is a free developer account and a little time. The table below summarizes which consoles work and what each requires.

Console Dev Mode Support Account Needed Notes
Xbox One Yes Partner Center developer account Original model; slower, but fully capable for retro emulation.
Xbox One S Yes Partner Center developer account Same toolchain as the original; a comfortable baseline.
Xbox One X Yes Partner Center developer account The most powerful One model; best for demanding emulators.
Xbox Series S / X Yes Partner Center developer account Same official path; noticeably more headroom.

The Account

The gateway to Developer Mode is a Microsoft Partner Center developer account, registered against your existing Microsoft account. As of June 2025, individual onboarding is free, which removed the old one-time registration fee that used to deter hobbyists. You complete the sign-up in a web browser, agree to the developer terms, and your account is ready shortly afterward.

The Activation App

On the console itself, you install the free Dev Mode Activation app from the Microsoft Store. It generates a code that you pair with your registered account, and then it switches the console into the developer sandbox. This is the moment your Xbox stops behaving like a normal retail unit and starts accepting sideloaded apps.

What Stays the Same

Activating Developer Mode does not erase your games or saves, and it does not void anything permanently. The retail and developer environments are kept separate, and you can switch back at any time. Think of it as a second mode that lives alongside the normal one rather than a replacement for it.
